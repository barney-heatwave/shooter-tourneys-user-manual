Half-Life: Zombies Ate My Neighbours Multiplayer

User Manual


About

ZAMNHLMP uses the award-winning Half-Life Engine, also known as GoldSource by Valve for hectic deathmatch games. ZAMNHLMP is also based from the codeframe of Aura which is a modification of Adrenaline Gamer by BulliT and uses open-source technologies by YaLTeR (OpenAG) for its client to give you the best experience.

Gamemodes

List of Gamemodes

  • Arcade: Gives the player all weapons and suit armour. Player regains suit armour and all weapons after killing another player.
  • Arena: Similar to Arcade, but players take turns 1v1-ing each other.
  • Crowbar Arena: Similar to Arena, but players only have the Crowbar.
  • Classic DM: Classic Deathmatch. 'Nuff said.
  • Capture the Flag
  • Capture the Flag 2: CTF, but with modified settings.
  • Free for All: Similar to Classic DM, but with more weapons.
  • Instant Gib: One-Shot Kills
  • Last Man Standing: Will you survive?
  • Last Team Standing: Will your team survive?
  • Team Deathmatch

Changing Gamemodes

Changing gamemodes on your server/game is not very hard to do and can be done using the console.

Each gamemode has their own CFG file which executes a bunch of commands and does all the hard work for you. Below are all the gamemode CFG files in the same order as the list above:

  • arcade
  • arena
  • cbar
  • cbcup
  • classic
  • ctf
  • ctf2
  • ffa
  • instagib
  • lms
  • lts
  • tdm

To change gamemodes, use the server console and type sv_aura_gamemode and then the name of the gamemode CFG file.

You can also choose to changelevel at the same time. Example:

sv_aura_gamemode classic; changelevel zamnhlmp_here

Creating Gamemodes

Creating simple gamemodes is simple. Go to the gamemodes folder where Shooter Tourneys is located, and you can make a duplicate of one of the existing gamemodes and open using Notepad or your text editor of choice (Notepad++ recommended). Changing things in there should be pretty much straight forward, since everything is commented and beginner-friendly. Note that sv_aura_gametype is very important, because it determines what gametype your gamemode will be based from. Arena is taking turns 1v1, CTF is, well, CTF, and these apply for LMS and LTS too. If you just want a simple gamemode, you can set the gametype to ffa.

Creating a gametype is hard to do and requires knowledge of the AG SDK. You can get AG SDK for Shooter Tourneys on GitHub.
